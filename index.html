Parameters

Population: This is the total population of people in the simulation
Infected: This is the number of infected people at the start of the simulation
Transmission Probability: This is the chance that the virus spreads if two people come in contact
Contact Rate: This is the number of contacts every person has on average in one day
Incubation Period: This is the average number of days during which the virus is present but not infectious or showing symptoms
Recovery Rate: This is the rate at which people recover/die from the virus on average - measured in 1/number of days


COVID- 19 is a pandemic that struck humanity at an alarming rate. Variables change on a dime with new statistics being reported after every minute - outbreak. Using conventional statistics to estimate the data is neither very accurate nor very easy to use.

SEIR stands for Susceptible, Exposed, Infectious and Recovered and uses differential equations to predict how the population shifts as the rates of moving from one population to another change. We can not derive the exact values by nature of differential equations themselves, but we can predict them to a good degree, as we have done here.
